History and Culture of Pune City

The various historical sites in Pune are:

Sinhagad Fort

Location: Sinhagad Ghat Road, Thoptewadi, Maharashtra.

Price: Rs, 20 per adult.

Timings: Every day 9:00 am to 6:00 pm.

Located around 35 kilometers from Pune, this majestic 2000-year-old fort is one of the most important historical places in Pune. The fort has witnessed numerous battles like the 1670 battle of Sinhagad. Named after the saint Kaundinya, it has changed hands between Koli, Peshwa, and Mughal kings. It is located atop a plateau in the Sahyadri Mountains and houses the Kaundinya Eshwar Temple, some beautifully carved hidden caves, bastions, and tombs of Rajaram Chhatrapati and Tanaji Malusare. Don’t miss to see the majestic Pune and Kalyan Darwaza, a brewery, and bunkers.

Shaniwar Wada

Location: Bajorao Road, Shaniwar Peth, Pune, Maharashtra.

Price:

  • Indians – ₹ 5 per person
  • Foreign Nationals – ₹ 125 per person

Timings: 08:00 am to 06:30 pm; every day

Symbols of Maratha pride and grandiosity, this is a 17th-century residential fort that was built by Peshwa Bajirao. Once you step inside, you are greeted with imposing architecture with rich Mughal influences spread over 600 acres. Its famous doors include Dilli Darwaza, Khidki Darwaza, Ganesh Darwaza, and Mastani Darwaza, used by Bajirao's companion.

Aga Khan Palace

Location: Gandhi National Memorial Society, Aga Khan Palace Road, Pune

Price:

  • Adults – ₹ 5 per person
  • Children – ₹ 2 per person
  • Foreign Nationals – ₹ 100 per person

Timings: 09:00 am to 05:30 pm; every day

Aga Khan Palace is a majestic building reflecting the marvel of Italian architecture and is surrounded by beautiful sprawling gardens. Visiting the palace gives you a glimpse into the majestic life of the royals. It was built in 1892 by Sultan Muhammad Shah Aga Khan III. The palace is associated with the Indian freedom movement as Mahatma Gandhi and his wife were imprisoned here.

Lal Mahal

Location: Kasba Peth, Pune

Price: Free

Timings: 09:00 am to 01:00 pm and 04:00 pm to 08:00 pm; every day

Lal Mahal is one of the most famous monuments located in Pune and was built by Shahaji Bhosale, father of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Shivaji Maharaj spent his childhood at Lal Mahal, and the place has a significant historical importance as it was the site of many important events in Shivaji's life.

Vishrambaug Wada

Location: RB Kumthekar Rd, Perugate, Sadashiv Peth, Pune, Maharashtra.

Price: Free

Timings: Every day 10:00 am to 06:00 pm.

Vishrambaug Wada is an iconic historical place in Pune. Built in 1807, it was the luxurious residence of Peshwa Bajirao II. The three-story mansion is a fine example of the architectural brilliance of the Peshwas, with ornate woodwork and intricate carvings.

Rajgad Fort

Location: Balekilla, Pune, Maharashtra.

Price: Free

Timings: Every day 9:00 am to 6:00 pm.

Rajgad Fort is another significant historical site located near Pune. It was the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj for almost 26 years. The fort is known for its strategic design and is a popular trekking destination for adventure enthusiasts. The breathtaking views from the top and the history associated with the fort make it a must-visit.

About

Offering a complete package of rich culture and ancient heritage, there are numerous historical places in Pune that entice tourists of all ages and provide a perfect backdrop for clicking pictures and selfies. From ancient forts, palaces, wadas to famous temples to a war memorial, all of these heritage monuments are impressive and boast of their significance and appeal.

All these historical monuments in Pune are marvellous and artistic and have managed to preserve the bygone-era magic and proudly exhibit the rich legacy that the city owns.
